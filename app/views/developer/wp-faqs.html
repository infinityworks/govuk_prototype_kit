Frequently Asked Questions

What is the NHS Apps Library?

The NHS Apps Library hosts a list of links to health and well-being apps which have been assessed by regulatory and NHS subject matter experts. It is designed to give patients, the public and healthcare professionals, access to trusted products that enable users to take increased control of an individual’s healthcare.

The apps are grouped by category to reflect overall function (diabetes, cancer, healthy living etc) and the library will expand over time as more apps are developed, assessed and published.

Will there be a separate library of apps for health professionals?

The apps listed on our library are for use by patients, members of the public and health professionals to help improve health and well-being outcomes.

What is the process for getting the app assessed and onto the NHS Apps Library?

If your app or digital tool is already in public use, it can be submitted for pre-assessment by regulatory and NHS subject matter experts. You will then be invited to answer our Digital Assessment Questions to the required standards before your product is published in the library.

Examples of the type of questions you will need to answer can be found here.

How can I develop an app that will be considered for the NHS Apps Library?

Our current focus is on assessing apps that address health themes with the most significant need. These are social care, cancer, mental health, maternity and long-term conditions like diabetes and asthma.

However, we are working on a framework of national standards that can be used to develop your product. In the meantime, please consult the Digital Assessment Questions for guidance on what criteria you will need to meet before getting published on our Apps Library.

Do I have to pay for my app to be assessed?

No. The assessment process is completely free.

What are the standard rules and regulations for health apps?

Information on the assessment criteria for health apps from Public Health England can be found here .

How long will the assessment take?

The timeframe will differ depending on how your app performs against the assessment criteria.

Do you provide funding for developing my app?

NHS Digital is keen to promote innovations already in the public domain. We want to assess your app and showcase it on our library, but we don’t offer funding. Find out more about commissioning in the NHS on the NHS England website.

Once my app is assessed, can I use the NHS logo?

Inclusion in our Apps Library does not automatically entitle you to use NHS branding. It can only be used where a third party has been commissioned to provide NHS services. Please refer to the NHS identity guidelines for further information about the use of the NHS brand.

Do I need to design my product to work on a specific platform?

Your app must work on one or more of the Android, IOS and Windows platforms.

Do you have a co-working space or developer user group?

We are currently enhancing the functionality of this website to allow us to host an online developer community, but we don’t offer access to a co-working space. If you wish to know more about how our project is progressing, register your interest here.

Can we meet to talk in more detail about our product?

NHS Digital is working hard to improve the assessment process. Once your assessment is underway, we will contact you if more information is required for your product to satisfy our Digital Assessment Questions. However, we don’t offer a bespoke consultancy service.

Can the assessment framework be used by commissioners and healthcare professionals to measure the effectiveness of apps they wish to use?

No. Our assessment process may require several NHS bodies and partners to identify an app’s effectiveness, so the Digital Assessment Questions cannot be used as a standalone assessment.

How do I find an app or digital tool for a specific purpose?

You can currently filter a search of the apps published in our library by condition/area of interest such as mental health, diabetes and healthy living.

Are apps on the library free?

Apps developed via Public Health England and the NHS are free, but those produced by private companies may carry a charge.
